Foreword

As I developed this theory that can explain all things, I was long troubled by a phenomenon: humans have created so many seemingly profound and popular terms—emergence, self-reference, self-organization, evolution, creation, generation, system, structure, and so on. They may carry weight in their respective fields, but the deeper I thought, the more I felt that none of them reached the ultimate truth. They only describe phenomena, processes, and results, yet never touch the most original, fundamental, and irreplaceable driving force. They explain how something appears, but not one of them truly answers why it appears on its own.

Then I returned to a seemingly ordinary yet universally overlooked term in my mother tongue: Ziran (Nature).

I suddenly realized that in Chinese, “Ziran” is never merely a noun. It does not mean the external natural world of mountains, rivers, plants, and trees. It is a concept with profound philosophical depth, even functioning as a verb. “Zi” means self; “Ran” means so, as it is, the original state. Combined, it means: being so by itself, becoming by itself, evolving by itself, generating by itself, existing by itself. No external force is needed, no deliberate design, no command, no explanation.

This is an extremely concise yet transparent worldview: all things are as they are, and the world moves on its own.

I began to check all major languages worldwide—English, French, German, Spanish, Russian, Greek—and was astonished to find that no language has a single word that fully matches the complete meaning of the Chinese word “Ziran”. In English, “nature” only refers to the physical world, an objective collection of things, without the dynamic sense of “being so by itself”. “Spontaneous” only means unplanned, “self-organization” is technical jargon, “automatic” means mechanical. All are shallow, one-sided, and lack philosophical root and living spirit.

This is a stunning truth: the most profound philosophical concept of humanity exists only in Chinese, yet cannot be precisely translated to the rest of the world.

What strengthened my conviction further is the greatest classic of the East: Tao Te Ching. According to public statistics, Tao Te Ching is the second most translated and published cultural classic in the world, after only the Bible. It is Eastern wisdom that has truly spread globally. Laozi clearly stated: Humans follow earth, earth follows heaven, heaven follows Tao, Tao follows Ziran.

This sentence reveals the ultimate law: Tao is already the law of all things, yet Tao still takes Ziran as its foundation. Ziran is the essence of Tao, the ultimate of the cosmos.

“Ziran” can be great or small, deep or shallow. It explains a speck of dust and the entire universe. Yet it is too ordinary, too broad—everyone uses it, but few truly understand it. I needed a unique, exclusive, irreplaceable term dedicated to this fundamental law. Not an academic term, not a trendy phrase, but a genesis-level symbol.

Then a thought arose, naturally: Use my name — Haoran.

Hao means vast, infinite, boundless dimensions—high dimensions infinitely high, low dimensions infinitely low. Ran means Ziran, being as it is, being so by itself, the truest way all things exist.

Haoran is exactly the soul of this entire theory.

I must be honest: this is not about self-promotion or seeking fame. On the contrary, I started observing from the tiny black spot on a clean toilet, the frost patterns on a window; I began questioning from the “axioms as non-negotiable rules” I could not bear in school; I reflected from the limits of absolute zero and the speed of light; I resonated with the wisdom of Laozi. I only walked forward, following the way the world truly is.

Then this theory took shape on its own. And its form is exactly the meaning of my name.

I did not invent it. I am only the first to clearly articulate it.

Therefore, I formally name this ultimate fundamental theory that runs through the cosmos, civilization, life, consciousness, and AI:

**Haoran Theory**

I have created a new English word — Haoran — making it the first and only word in the world that fully expresses: self-existing, self-rooted, infinitely unfolding, naturally being so.

It belongs to no discipline, no culture. It is the law of the cosmos itself.

I just happen to share the same name.
