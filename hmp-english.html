Abstract

This paper proposes the HaoRan Meta-Principle (HMP), a unified meta-theoretical framework for describing existential structure, dimensional generation, state transformation, and systemic evolution. Independent of specific physical constants, experimental predictions, and microdynamic equations, HMP reveals the common generative logic underlying various phenomena from a structural perspective. Based on two fundamental existential forms—the 0-State (unobservable substrate structure) and 1-State (observable manifested structure)—and three core axioms, this framework provides a unified interpretive pathway for phenomena such as light, the light speed boundary, dark matter indications, energy conservation, life emergence, cognitive phase transition, social stratification, and wealth generation. HMP does not propose new physical entities but offers a structural interpretive framework.

Without negating or replacing existing scientific systems, HMP serves as an underlying structural meta-framework that provides a unified logical foundation for interdisciplinary integration. This paper aims to construct a self-consistent, closed, compatible, and broadly explanatory meta-theoretical system, offering new insights for the unification of human cognition.

Chapter 1: Introduction

1.1 Research Background

Contemporary scientific and knowledge systems exhibit highly specialized and fragmented characteristics: physics studies spacetime, fields, and matter; biology investigates life, evolution, and emergence; social sciences explore organization, behavior, and structure; philosophy examines existence, ontology, and cognition. The lack of a unified underlying language across disciplines results in insufficient cross-domain explanatory power.

Existing unified theories are mostly built on high-dimensional geometry, quantum gravity, or mathematical formalization, making it difficult to cover complex systems such as life, society, and cognition. Therefore, constructing an interdisciplinary, structural, non-intrusive unified meta-theory holds significant academic and cognitive value.

1.2 Research Objectives

  • Construct a self-consistent, closed, and minimalist meta-theoretical structure.
  • Provide a unified interpretive logic covering physics, biology, society, and cognition.
  • Avoid conflicts with or replacement of existing disciplinary systems, achieving underlying compatibility.
  • Offer a unified structural model for complex systems, emergence, phase transitions, and ontology.

1.3 Research Significance

HMP provides top-level structural guidance for fragmented knowledge systems, enabling natural sciences, social sciences, and cognitive systems to achieve logical coherence at the underlying level and promoting human cognition toward higher levels of unity.
